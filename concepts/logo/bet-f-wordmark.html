The wordmark.

Fashion maisons rarely lead with symbols. They lead with their names — set with absolute precision, carved into stone, stamped on spines, engraved on glass above shop doors. YSL, Dior, Margiela, Comme des Garçons: the typography is the identity.

The lucky accident: Taylor and Maison are both six letters. Stacked tight and mathematically locked to identical width, they form a perfect block — a single shape, not two words. One line on top of the other, zero ornament, zero symbol. The name is the logo.
