The crop mark.

Every designer knows these — the two L-shaped brackets that define a trim edge before a page goes to print. What's inside is what you chose to keep. Everything else was cut. Taylor's logo is the frame of consideration itself — the most insider editorial mark possible.

The idea: a creative director's job is judgment — knowing what to keep, knowing what to cut. Crop marks are the most intimate tool of that craft. Using them as the identity says "I am the frame" without ever speaking. Designers know what they mean; clients feel the taste.

Variations

The mark has a family — two corners, four corners, or a frame around the wordmark as a lockup.

Two-corner
The primary mark. Upper-left + lower-right.
Four-corner
Full registration frame. Seals, invoices, letterheads.
With TM lockup
Corners frame the initials. Headers, signage.
