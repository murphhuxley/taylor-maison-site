Design sheet · Taylor Maison · 2026

The identity system.

A maison identity runs on two marks doing two jobs. The wordmark is the voice — carved, permanent, unmistakable — and goes wherever the full name fits. The pearl is the seal — a single still glyph that carries the brand where the wordmark can't: favicon, avatar, sign-off, stamp. Together they cover every surface the brand will ever touch.

The wordmark · primary Cabinet Grotesk 800 · stacked · equal-width
The pearl · anchor Two circles · disc + subtractive highlight
Role · 01 · Primary

The wordmark carries the name.

"The house, named."

Both words locked to identical width via SVG textLength. The stack forms a single typographic block — not two words but one shape. This is the default face of the brand; it shows up everywhere the wordmark fits, and that's most surfaces.

  • Site header and nav
  • Homepage masthead
  • Case study titles
  • Business card fronts
  • Invoices, letterheads, legal
  • Email signature header
  • Deck covers and handoffs
Role · 02 · Anchor

The pearl carries the signature.

"The seal the house stamps."

A single glyph for the places the wordmark can't live — browser tabs, profile avatars, app icons, the end of an email. Built with two circles only: a solid disc plus a smaller, hard-edged highlight that uses the background color. Deliberately still — the calm counterweight to the ferrofluid orb's motion on the homepage.

  • Favicon + app icon
  • Social profile avatars
  • Email sign-off mark
  • Business card reverse
  • Watermark on images + exports
  • Closing stamp on documents
  • Stationery seal

Lockups

When the two marks appear together

Most of the time, the marks don't meet — F lives on wordmark surfaces, B lives on glyph surfaces. Lockups are ceremonial. Use them on letterheads, formal correspondence, certificates, deck covers — places where the brand is signing something. Three approved configurations.

Lockup A · Stacked seal

Pearl above wordmark, centered. The canonical lockup — reads as coat-of-arms or couture label. Use on letterheads, deck covers, certificates.

Lockup B · Horizontal

Pearl left of wordmark, vertically centered. For horizontal contexts: narrow headers, invoice tops, email-signature header rows.

Lockup C · Rule
TAYLOR MAISON EST. MMXXV

Wordmark set inline, thin rule, pearl, establishment line. For formal ceremonies: awards, signed correspondence, imprimatur.

On charcoal

The full system inverted

Every mark has an inverse. The wordmark holds at any size on charcoal; the pearl uses the dark variant where the highlight circle matches the charcoal background instead of cream.

Rules of the system

Four rules, one principle. The marks survive through restraint, not decoration. Any treatment that "spices them up" breaks the thing that makes them maison-grade.

Rule · 01

Pick one mark per surface.

Never show the wordmark and pearl in the same space unless it's one of the three approved lockups. Parallel placement reads as competition, not system.

Rule · 02

Honor the minimum size.

Wordmark: 80px wide minimum. Pearl: 16px minimum. Below these sizes the marks start to erode — especially the equal-width lock on the wordmark.

Rule · 03

Clear space = the M-height.

Around any placement, reserve clear space equal to the cap-height of the letter M in the wordmark. This applies to both marks. No text, image, or UI element touches inside that zone.

Rule · 04

Only two colorways: cream + charcoal.

Charcoal marks on cream backgrounds, cream marks on charcoal backgrounds. No silver, no gold, no accent-color variants. The brand is monochrome. (Deliberate: Taylor explicitly dislikes gold.)

Don't.

Four failures to avoid.

No gradients Don't soften.
No outlines Don't hollow.
No stretching Don't distort.
No rotation Don't tilt.
