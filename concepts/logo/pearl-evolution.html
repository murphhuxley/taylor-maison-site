Pearl · Four evolutions

Round 2 · 2026-04-18

The original pearl is clean, but Lens returns a flood of near-identical music apps, lighting products, and utility icons — the "solid disc + offset highlight" is a shared visual primitive, not a distinctive mark. Each evolution below keeps the app-icon-appropriate cleanliness (proving the UI/UX voice) while adding one specific move that breaks the generic match. Pick a direction.

0 · Original

For reference — not a candidate

The starting point. Two circles. The Lens results show why we're moving.

1 · Crescent

The move: drop the highlight, cut a crescent. Pearl becomes a moon-phase — same primitive family, but specifically asymmetric. Reads as motion arrested, not a symmetric app icon.
Why it works: removes the thing every music app does. Retains the "clean UI" language. Gains a name.
Watch: at 16px the crescent can feel thin — may need the cut tuned tighter.

2 · Threshold

Half-stamped, half-empty
16
32

The move: pearl split vertically. Left half is solid ink, right half is outline. Dot crosses the seam — the part on the outline side is outlined too, the part on the solid side is void.
Why it works: ties directly to maison — a threshold, a door half-open, inside/outside. The split is the concept.
Watch: stroke thinness is fragile at 16px; may need a solid-only micro-variant for tiny scale.

3 · Seal

Pressed, with a nick
16
32

The move: keep disc + highlight, add one triangular notch cut into the right edge. The nick is the signature — a pressed wax-seal imperfection.
Why it works: minimal deviation from the original, preserves 99% of the recognizability we built, but kills the pixel-match with app icons. A single imperfection tells Lens "not that."
Watch: at 16px the notch may disappear — OK if favicon has a "flattened" version.

4 · Orbit

The move: the small dot is no longer inside the body — it's outside, upper-right. Two solid elements in gravitational relationship, not one circle with a void.
Why it works: mirrors the Ferrofluid orb's "something orbits the main body" logic. Completely breaks the eye/iris/aperture read that every app icon shares.
Watch: compositionally more complex, uses more real estate. Less suited to square-crop avatars unless we commit to the full rectangular canvas.

5 · Stamped · bonus

Monogram hidden inside

The move: replace the highlight dot with a lowercase t in the negative space. Same silhouette as the original pearl — but now there's proprietary type inside. Impossible to match.
Why it works: unmistakably yours the moment anyone reads it. Still reads as a "pearl shape" from far away, reads as a signed seal up close.
Watch: the t becomes a shape blur at favicon scale — may need a simpler solid-dot variant for 16-24px contexts.
