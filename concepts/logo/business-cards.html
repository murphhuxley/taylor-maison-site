Business cards · explorations

Six ways to put it on a card.

Standard US 3.5″ × 2″. Every concept renders front + back at proportional scale, so what you see is the printed object. The logo is rendered from a vector recreation; we'll swap in your production SVG before going to print. Voice A (heavy tight) is used as the wordmark default — say the word and I'll re-render any concept in B (editorial) or C (wide-tracked).

Material assumptions: heavy uncoated stock (Mohawk Superfine ~130lb cover or Colorplan), 2-color (cream + charcoal). Premium options noted per concept where they'd land.

01

Quiet mark, loud whitespace

The cream front is mostly negative space. Logo small in the top-left, contact info small in the bottom-right. Back is the wordmark, large and confident, on the same cream. The card is a quiet object — it whispers, then commits.

The argument

Two opposites. Cream face is a poster of the mark. Charcoal face is a function-first business card. Hand someone the cream face when you want to be remembered; flip to charcoal when they ask for your details. The duotone print is a flex but a sensible one.
