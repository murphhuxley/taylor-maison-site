The dot.

B, stripped. No gradient, no highlight, no dimensionality — just a filled circle, which in every written language on earth is already a symbol: a period, a full stop, the end of a considered thought. The most universal mark humans have ever agreed on.

The mark is punctuation: "Taylor Maison" with a bold period after it. The dot isn't a logo pretending to be a symbol — it's a symbol the world already reads. That means it's also proprietary by association the moment it lives next to the name. Every time you end a sentence, his brand signs itself.
