The editorial serif.

Same hypothesis as F — the name is the mark — but told in a different voice. Where F speaks in Cabinet Grotesk's modern architectural sans, G borrows the language of magazine mastheads, perfume bottles, and literary spines. High-contrast Didone-adjacent letterforms: thin hairlines, decisive verticals, an inherited seriousness.

Why serif for a maison: YSL, Dior, Tom Ford, Proenza Schouler, The Row — every house that wants to say "couture, not contemporary" says it in serif. The question on this bet isn't "is this pretty" — it's "does Taylor Maison belong in that conversation, or next to Margiela / Comme / A.P.C.?" F is modern. G is inherited. One of those answers is more true.
