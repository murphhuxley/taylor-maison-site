The shortlist · your picks

A wordmark and an anchor.

Every serious maison runs this two-part system: a primary typographic mark (the name, set with precision) and a single abstract glyph (for favicon, avatar, sign-off, seal). Chanel: CHANEL + interlocking Cs. Hermès: HERMÈS + the Duc carriage. Margiela: MAISON MARGIELA + numbered tag. You picked the same shape of system instinctively.

Bet F · Wordmark · primary

The pearl.

Solid disc with subtractive highlight. Still, heavy, object-like — the calm counterweight to the orb's motion. The single glyph that carries the brand where the wordmark can't: favicon, avatar, sign-off, seal.

Open Bet B
Why this shape of system works: The orb on the homepage already carries "alive, dimensional, reactive." If the mark repeats that job, the brand speaks in one voice. Pairing a typographic primary (permanent, carved) with a still glyph (solid, object-like) gives Taylor Maison two registers — the house's name + the house's seal. That's a maison identity, not a logo.

Round 3 · alternatives to B and F

Each one explores a question the shortlist doesn't fully answer. G flips F's voice. H strips B to its essence. I bridges the two. None of these replace B + F — they test whether another version of that same system might fit better before we commit.

Bet G · Editorial serif

The couture fork of F.

"What if the name is set in a Didone instead?"

Same stacked equal-width architecture as F, but in a high-contrast serif (Gambarino). Trades modern/architectural for inherited/editorial — closer to Vogue mastheads and couture bottles than Margiela. The question: is Taylor Maison modern or inherited?

Open Bet G
Bet H · The dot

The pearl, stripped.

"The mark is a period."

B with the dimension removed — just a filled circle, which is already a universal symbol: a full stop. The dot becomes punctuation on the name ("taylormaison."). Most durable shape on earth, survives 8px to billboard. Recognizable by co-placement, not by form.

Open Bet H
Bet I · TM ligature

The bridge.

"One glyph, both letters, custom cut."

T is inscribed inside M. Three verticals share a top crossbar — outer two become M's legs, center is T's stem terminating at the M's apex. Contains the name's DNA like F, works at glyph scale like B, and no other studio can use it. The most proprietary of the three.

Open Bet I

How to decide.

Open each Round 3 bet in its own tab and compare against B + F. Three tests:

Does it beat F on voice?

G is the only real contender here. Is Taylor Maison's voice the architectural sans of Cabinet (contemporary, maison-as-studio) or the Didone inheritance of Gambarino (couture, maison-as-atelier)? Pick one.

Does it beat B as the anchor?

H (stripped dot) is simpler and more durable but less recognizable alone. I (TM ligature) is more proprietary but busier. B's pearl is the middle ground. The question: do you want the anchor glyph to carry meaning, or just carry presence?

Does any pair outperform B + F?

Mix and match in your head: F + H (cleanest), G + B (most couture), F + I (most proprietary), G + H (most editorial). One of those pairings is your identity. Or B + F stays.
