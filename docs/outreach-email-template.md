# Taylor Maison Creative — Cold Outreach Email Template
**Built:** 2026-03-10
**Purpose:** First-touch cold email to Hamptons business owners with weak/outdated websites
**Tone:** Personal, specific, no-fluff. Not a mass pitch.

---

## How to Use This

Every email should be customized with the **[BRACKETED FIELDS]** filled in from the outreach list.
The formula: name the specific problem, show you've actually looked at their business, make the ask small.

Do NOT send a wall of text. Do NOT mention your agency in the first sentence.
Do NOT say "I noticed your website could use some work." That's what every spam pitch says.

## Follow-Up Sequence

**Day 1:** Send initial email (above template)
**Day 4:** One-line follow-up if no reply:
> "Just bumping this up — happy to send a quick 3-minute Loom walkthrough of the specific issues I found if that's easier."

**Day 10:** Final touch if still no reply:
> "No worries if the timing isn't right. I'll be doing work for a few businesses in [their area] this spring — if you want to chat before summer, the door's open."

After that, move on. Don't pester.

---

## What NOT To Do

- Don't say "I came across your website" — it's generic
- Don't lead with your agency name or credentials
- Don't send the same email to 15 people — the specificity IS the pitch
- Don't include a portfolio deck in the first email — earn the next step first
- Don't ask "do you need a new website" — they'll say no. Name the problem, let them react.
