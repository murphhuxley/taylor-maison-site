## Strategy Notes

**The problem with most cold email:** It leads with what *you* do, not what *they're* missing.

**Taylor's angle:** Lead with observation. Prove you've looked at them specifically. Make the email feel like a warm introduction, not a pitch. End with a low-friction ask.

**Tone:** Confident but not arrogant. Like a friend who happens to be the best designer you've ever met, not a salesperson with a quota.

**Don't say:** "I noticed your website could use some improvement" — patronizing.
**Do say:** Something specific that proves you saw their site, know their business, and have an actual idea.

## Template A — Primary Template (Restaurant/Boutique)
*Use for: restaurants, boutiques, event companies*

**Subject lines (A/B test these):**
- `Re: [Business Name] this summer`
- `A thought on [Business Name]`
- `Quick question about [Business Name]`
- `Your [restaurant/shop] deserves better online`

---

**Email body:**

Hi [Name],

I've been a fan of [Business Name] for a while — [one specific, genuine detail about their business — a dish, the location, something you actually know].

I came across your website recently, and I had to reach out. What you've built in person doesn't quite come through online. The site doesn't communicate the [quality / atmosphere / experience] that anyone who's actually been there already knows.

## Follow-Up Sequence

**Day 1:** Send primary email
**Day 5:** One-line bump

> "Just following up on this — happy to share a quick visual concept for [Business Name] if you want to see what I'm thinking before getting on a call."

**Day 10:** Final touch

> "Closing the loop here — I'll be reaching out to a few [restaurants/shops/etc.] in [area] this summer, so wanted to give you first look. If timing is off right now, happy to reconnect when the season gets close."

## Do's and Don'ts

### DO:
- Reference something specific about their business in every email
- Offer to show something (a concept, an idea) — not just talk
- Keep it under 200 words for the primary email
- Use their first name
- Make the ask feel easy (15-min call, not "schedule a discovery session")

### DON'T:
- Say "I was browsing your website and noticed..."
- Use phrases like "digital footprint," "online presence optimization," or "conversion-focused"
- Attach a deck or case study PDF to a cold email
- Send from a generic gmail — use [taylor@taylormaison.com](mailto:taylor@taylormaison.com)
- Follow up more than 3 times without a response

## Personalization Checklist (Before Sending)

- [ ] I know the owner's first name (LinkedIn, website, Instagram)
- [ ] I've looked at their actual current website
- [ ] I have one specific observation about their business that proves I did the homework
- [ ] The email is under 200 words
- [ ] My portfolio site is live and the relevant case study is visible
- [ ] I have a specific ask (call, Zoom, coffee) with a time attached
