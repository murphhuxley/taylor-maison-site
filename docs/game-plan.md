## Operating principles

| Principle | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Do not productize the brand signal.** | No public flat-rate pricing on TM's homepage. No "no calls" policy. The conversation IS the trust motion. |
| **Do productize the delivery system.** | Repeatable SKUs, fixed scopes, internal rate card, defined onboarding, templated retainers, cadence-driven engagements. The customer never sees the engine. |
| **Quality wins trust. Speed wins confidence.** | Don't promise DesignJoy-disposable speed. Do reply within hours, send proposals within 24h, ship first drafts faster than any local competitor. Visible momentum is a differentiator. |
| **Close on business outcomes, not AI language.** | Sell "less admin, better follow-up, cleaner intake, faster proposals, calmer client experience." AI is the unfair capability — never the headline. |
| **Three real sales conversations per week, May–July.** | Hard floor. If you go a week without three, that's the alarm. Track in a single doc with a date column. |
| **Warm intros only.** | The Hamptons market runs on trust. Cold outreach poisons positioning. JB Andreassi is the priority intro. |

## The website's job

The site is **a trust-and-sales artifact** — not a lead-gen engine yet, but absolutely a sales tool. Its four functions:

1. **Ratify the warm intro.** Someone Googles you after a referral. Three seconds decides whether they take the meeting.
2. **Pre-sell taste.** The aesthetic itself is a credential. Locked dark redesign already does this work.
3. **Qualify seriousness.** No public pricing filters out tire-kickers; the contact motion (mailto, no form) signals "this is a conversation, not a transaction."
4. **Make the next conversation feel obvious.** Engagement copy + portfolio combine to leave the visitor thinking "yes, I want to talk to this person."

This is why the locked design is right: dark, bold, typography-forward, no prices, contact = orb + mailto. Build it. Ship it. Move on.

## Service architecture

### Public face (homepage Engagements section)

Three engagement blocks, no dollar signs:

| Engagement | Public framing | What it actually is |
|---|---|---|
| **Focused refresh** | For brands that need their digital presence to match their reputation. | Tier 1 — $3–5K, 4–6 page Next.js, 2-week delivery. |
| **Full build** | For businesses ready to treat their website as a brand asset. | Tier 2 — $8–12K, 8–12 pages, CMS, advanced motion, 4–6 weeks. |
| **Ongoing partnership** | For studios and operators who want a design + AI partner on call. | Productized retainer — $1.5–5K/mo, async-first cadence, defined output rhythm. |

The "Ongoing partnership" block is where the productized engine lives. It looks bespoke from the outside; it runs on templates, intake forms, weekly delivery rhythms internally.

### Premium tier (Tier 3, $15–25K)

Not on the homepage. Surfaces in the conversation. Includes intelligent systems layer (booking AI, automated workflows, custom tools, integrations). This is the bridge to forkable ops layer in 2027.

### Side service: 3D property scans

$1K each. Same-day delivery. **Trojan horse, not profit center.** Use to get into rooms with brokerages and luxury builders. Bundle 3 with every Tier 2 build. Don't try to scale property scan retainers until 5 paid website clients are shipped.

## The conversion machine

### The Three-Act Pitch (use every time)

**Act 1 — Trust opener:** "I grew up here. I know your clients."
**Act 2 — Reframe their site:** "Your website is the only part of your business operating at 2008 quality. When a $4M-listing client Googles you and lands on a Wix template, you lose authority before the conversation starts."
**Act 3 — The unfair capability (in business outcomes, not AI words):** "I can build you the back-end that means fewer 11pm inquiry emails, cleaner intake, automated follow-up, and a proposal flow that responds in minutes instead of days. Nobody else local can do this."

Never say "AI agency." Say "what your business needs."

### The proposal motion

- First conversation → proposal in 24 hours. One page. Not a deck.
- Always present Premium first, let them self-select down.
- 50% deposit. No work without it.
- Always close with a specific referral ask: *"Who's the other restaurateur in Southampton you respect most? Would you introduce me?"*

### The cadence (May–July only)

| Cadence | Target | Mechanism |
|---|---|---|
| Sales conversations | 3 per week minimum | Calendar block + Notion log |
| Proposals sent | 1 per week minimum | Triggered by every qualifying conversation |
| Closes | 2 per month avg | Mix of Starter + Standard, occasional Premium |
| Retainer pitches | 1 per delivered project | Built into project closeout |

If conversations drop below 3/week, that's the Dad Pattern reasserting itself through "infrastructure work." Course-correct immediately.

## Scaling path

### Phase 1 — Solo / Local / Project-based (Now → August)

**Don't:** hire, productize secondary offerings, build internal tooling, expand to NYC, take Suffolk clients unless they walk to you.
**Do:** close, deliver, collect testimonials, write case studies while fresh, photograph everything.

Concentration is the asset. Five reference clients in a 10-mile radius is the foundation for everything after.

### Phase 2 — Productize one secondary SKU (Aug → Dec)

**Pick: Maison Monthly** (productized "Ongoing partnership" retainer). Why this one:
- Stacks on every prior client (every shipped project is a renewal candidate)
- Bridges to Phase 3 (forkable ops layer)
- Doesn't require new audience or new positioning
- Recurring revenue smooths the seasonal Q4 dip

Defer Brand Identity Package and Property Scan Retainer to 2027. One productized SKU at a time.

### Phase 3 — Leverage (2027)

- **First hire:** junior dev, $4–6K/mo, contract-to-perm. Hire when $25K/mo recurring is real and you're personally turning down work.
- **NYC overflow:** 3–5 NYC agency relationships, 10–15% referral fee.
- **Forkable ops layer:** the moat. Each Premium client → recurring $5–10K/mo operations engagement.

The mistake to avoid: skipping Phase 1 to chase Phase 3. Forkable orgs without paying clients is masturbation. Sell first, automate second.

## Risks (read every Sunday)

1. **The Dad Pattern through agents.** Building Murph features instead of calling JB. Building tooling instead of selling. Real-world conversations always beat infrastructure work.
2. **Premium tier seduction.** Premium pays 5x but takes 5x longer to close. Don't stop selling Starters because you're chasing one Premium.
3. **Scope creep on the first 3 clients.** They'll ask for the moon. Set hard scope in the proposal — two revisions, then change orders.
4. **Trying to be DesignJoy.** You're not. Don't poison positioning by productizing too publicly too early.
5. **The Q4 gap.** Summer ends, half the businesses close for off-season. You need retainer revenue locked by Aug 31 or Q4 hurts. Plan for it now.
