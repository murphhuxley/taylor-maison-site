# Hamptons Event & Wedding Venues — Website Audit
**Research Cycle 4 | Taylor Maison Creative**
**Date:** March 10, 2026
**Researcher:** Murph Huxley

---

## Executive Summary

The Hamptons wedding and event market is enormous — venues charge $20K–$100K+ per event and cater to an out-of-town clientele that makes virtually all decisions online before ever visiting. **These websites are the primary sales tool.** The problem: most are terrible at that job.

Common failures across this market:
- No pricing transparency (forces cold phone calls, kills momentum)
- Static photo galleries vs. modern immersive visual experiences
- No virtual tours despite venues selling on aesthetics
- Weak/missing inquiry funnels (phone number only, or buried contact forms)
- Mobile experiences that feel like 2014
- No social proof integration (Instagram, real wedding galleries)

**Total pipeline estimate (realistic first-year take rate): $120K–$200K**
**Seasonal urgency: Wedding season starts May 2026 — decision window is NOW**

## SECTION 4: MARKET OBSERVATIONS

### The Core Problem
Out-of-town brides plan from 6–18 months out and make venue decisions primarily online. The Hamptons wedding market competes directly with Newport, Cape Cod, Hudson Valley, and Nantucket — all of which have venues with significantly more sophisticated web presences.

### The Seasonal Urgency Play
- **May 2026 wedding season starts in ~8 weeks**
- Venues booking 2026 summer dates are finalizing NOW
- The sales pitch writes itself: "If a bride searches 'Hamptons vineyard wedding' today and can't find you — or worse, finds a broken/dated site — you've lost that wedding."

### Patterns Worth Noting
1. **Venues with corporate parents (Oceanbleu/CulinArt, Southampton Social Club) tend to have the worst standalone identity** — major opportunity for custom web presence
2. **Private clubs (BTSC) are allergic to marketing** — but they still sell events; the gap is enormous
3. **Catering companies consistently undersell themselves** — great food, terrible websites
4. **The PDF brochure / Flickr pattern** (Solé East) suggests operators who haven't updated their web strategy since 2012

### Competitive Insight
A handful of venues (Topping Rose, Ram's Head Inn) have invested in modern web presence. They are booking consistently. The correlation is not subtle.
