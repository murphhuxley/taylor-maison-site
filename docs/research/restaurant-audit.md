# Hamptons Restaurant Website Audit
**Taylor Maison Creative — Prospect Research**
**Cycle 1 | March 10, 2026**

---

## Overview

Audited 10 restaurants across Taylor's home turf (Southampton, Sag Harbor, Montauk) plus a few notable East Hampton/Bridgehampton spots flagged in the 2026 Social Life Magazine guide. Graded each website, identified specific issues, estimated project value, and noted warm intro angles.

**Grade Scale:**
- 🟢 **Modern** — clean, mobile-first, current design, booking works
- 🟡 **Dated** — functional but aging, missing conversion features
- 🔴 **Terrible** — embarrassing relative to the restaurant's prestige/price point

### 2. El Verano
**URL:** elveranony.com
**Grade:** 🟡 Dated
**Platform:** IONOS/DM Web Builder (their "created by MCV© 2026" footer — MCV is a basic web builder)
**Booking:** Resy button embedded (links to a 2024 date hardcoded in the URL — dead link)

**Issues:**
- Resy booking button points to `date=2024-04-27` — that date has long passed, likely broken flow
- Homepage content is literally one paragraph and hours — almost nothing else
- No photos, no menu preview, no story
- The "NBC Latino quote" appears twice on the same page (copypaste error)
- Site feels like a launch placeholder from 2023 that was never updated
- SSL: ✅ (redirects properly)
- Mobile: minimal but functional due to DM builder

**Project Value:** $8,000–15,000 for a full redesign with proper Resy integration, gallery, menu, and brand storytelling. This is a $$$$ restaurant with upscale crowd — the site is wildly mismatched to the price point.

**Warm Intro:** Jobs Lane / Agawam Park corner location — prime Southampton real estate. Chef Julian Medina is an NYC transplant without local roots. Taylor's Southampton upbringing = authentic local credibility. Strong pitch angle: "this neighborhood deserves a site that matches the room."

### 3. Donohue's (Southampton location)
**URL:** No dedicated Hamptons website found
**Grade:** 🔴 Terrible (no site)
**Platform:** N/A

**Notes:**
- The NYC original (845 Lexington Ave) has a basic site at donohuessteakhouse.com but it's NYC-only

### 4. Fēniks
**URL:** feniks.com
**Grade:** 🟡 Dated (with critical errors)
**Platform:** Squarespace
**Booking:** Tock (exploretock.com)

**Issues:**
- **Page title is literally "Your Site Title | Book Your Table Now"** — Squarespace placeholder was never updated. Appears in browser tab and Google search results. This is embarrassing for a fine dining restaurant that took over the legendary Plaza Cafe space.
- Site is essentially one page with just hours, an address, and Tock embed
- No About section, no menu gallery, no chef bio, no story about the Greek/Asian/Croatian fusion concept
- "CLOSED FOR VACATION · REOPENING FEB" — still showing winter status in March
- The restaurant has a genuinely exciting concept (Chef Douglas Gulija, 8-course tasting menu, chef's counter) that the website completely fails to communicate
- SSL: ✅

**Project Value:** $10,000–18,000. This is the best cold pitch on this list. They have incredible content to tell (legacy building, innovative chef, unique location on Jobs Lane) and zero digital storytelling. The gap between quality of restaurant and quality of website is maximum here.

**Warm Intro:** 75 Jobs Lane — heart of Southampton Village, Taylor's literal backyard. The Plaza Cafe ran there for decades; Fēniks took it over in summer 2025. Local credibility play: "I grew up 5 blocks from here, the building deserves a website that matches what you're doing inside."

## Sag Harbor

---

### 5. The American Hotel
**URL:** theamericanhotel.com
**Grade:** 🟡 Dated
**Platform:** WordPress + Elementor
**Booking:** Resy (integrated but linking to a 2024 hardcoded date: `?date=2024-07-15`)

**Issues:**
- WordPress/Elementor combo — functional but not optimized
- Same dead Resy date-link issue as El Verano (pointing to July 2024)
- Primary image on the restaurant page references Elementor's placeholder PNG — the actual hero image failed to load
- 85-page wine list is a bragging point but no digital menu or wine list preview
- Design feels old-money appropriate (which may be intentional) but the tech stack is creaking
- SSL: ✅
- Facebook is the only social link visible

**Project Value:** $12,000–20,000. This is a delicate pitch — the American Hotel has existed since the 1700s and their clientele expects tradition over flash. The angle isn't "let's modernize you" — it's "your current site has broken booking links and placeholder images that are actively losing you reservations."

**Warm Intro:** This is the most prestigious Sag Harbor institution. The Howard Marks estate management world absolutely intersects here — this is where that crowd dines. "I spent summers managing estates for clients who ate here weekly" is a strong door-opener.

### 6. Page (Lola Burr)
**URL:** pagesagharbor.com
**Grade:** 🔴 Terrible
**Platform:** Appears to be a custom-built or low-end web builder
**Booking:** No clear booking integration found on the page

**Issues:**
- **Literally says "MOBILE VERSION UNDER CONSTRUCTION"** with Lorem ipsum placeholder text visible on the mobile version
- The site returns HTTP 403 Forbidden when accessed without the www prefix — broken DNS/server config
- No booking widget visible on the homepage
- Menu links (MENU, DRINKS MENU) exist but the mobile experience is completely broken
- The site was apparently designed to only be "viewed on Laptop/Desktop PC" — this is their actual message on mobile
- OpenTable listing exists (`Page at 63 Main`) suggesting they may rely entirely on OT for bookings
- SSL: Technically present but mixed with 403 issues

**Project Value:** $8,000–14,000. Sag Harbor's creative class crowd is 80% mobile when searching for dinner on a Friday evening. A restaurant that tells mobile users to get a laptop is leaving reservations on the table.

**Warm Intro:** The restaurant's story (built on whaling ship limestone from Sag Harbor's past) is genuinely compelling — and it's buried. Taylor's Sag Harbor familiarity + creative industry positioning = natural fit.

---

### 7. Sen Restaurant
**URL:** senrestaurant.com
**Grade:** 🟡 Dated
**Platform:** SpotOn (restaurant POS/web platform)
**Booking:** Toast online ordering; no inline reservation widget found

**Issues:**
- SpotOn's web template is utilitarian — not terrible but clearly templated
- Lots of customer reviews displayed (5-star Google reviews embedded), which is a bit cluttered
- No clear "Reserve a Table" CTA above the fold
- Ordering goes to Toast (toasttab.com) — fine for delivery/pickup, but doesn't capture dine-in reservations inline
- The page title is generic ("The Hamptons, Sag Harbor, NY") — poor for SEO
- Photos are referenced via SpotOn CDN — limited control over visual presentation
- SSL: ✅ (HTTP/1.1 — not HTTP/2, slightly older infra)

**Project Value:** $6,000–10,000. They've been a Sag Harbor mainstay since 1994 and their reputation (Eric Ripert eats here) exceeds their digital presence. Not the sexiest pitch but a solid one.

**Warm Intro:** Year-round restaurant, Sag Harbor institution. Taylor's year-round Hamptons presence (May-Sept) means he'll be a neighbor. "The person Le Bernardin's chef comes to on the East End deserves better than a SpotOn template."
