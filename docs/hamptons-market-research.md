## The Market Reality

The Hamptons is NOT just a summer market anymore. Post-pandemic, the "seasonal exodus from NYC" created a year-round economy. Commercial real estate is active, restaurants stay open off-season, and businesses need digital presence 12 months a year — not just for Memorial Day through Labor Day.

**Key insight:** Businesses that used to survive on foot traffic alone now need websites that work year-round. The pandemic proved that. But most haven't upgraded.

## Competitive Landscape — Who Taylor Is Actually Competing Against

### Tier 1: The Local Incumbent
**Hamptons Web Design** (hamptonswebdesign.com) — Sag Harbor
- Operating since 1998. 20+ year relationships with local businesses.
- Services: website design, SEO, Google AdWords, hosting, email
- Pricing: "Affordable" — likely $1,500-$5,000 range
- Tech: WordPress, basic CMS
- Portfolio: functional but dated. Think 2015-era responsive WordPress.
- Strengths: Deep local trust, long client relationships, responsive service
- Weaknesses: No modern animation, no Next.js, no AI, design quality is functional not premium
- **Taylor's angle:** You're not competing with John for his existing clients. You're going after the clients who've outgrown what he offers.

### Tier 2: The Full-Service Shops
**blumenfeld + fleming** (bplusf.com) — Montauk
- Branding, graphic design, web design, advertising
- Their own site runs on Cloudways WordPress
- Positioned as "the Hamptons firm" — branding + web + marketing
- Likely $5,000-$15,000 for web projects
- **Taylor's angle:** They're a traditional agency. You build things they can't.

**Italia Designs** (italiadesigns.nyc)
- NYC-based, serves Long Island + Hamptons
- Full menu: web design, SEO, social media, branding, print, outdoor advertising, media buying
- Wix-built site. Services every vertical.
- Classic generalist agency — jack of all trades
- **Taylor's angle:** They spread thin. You go deep on premium web + AI.

**Graphic Image Group** — East Hampton
- John Musnicki, signage + vehicle lettering + web design
- More of a print shop that also does websites
- Not a real competitor at the premium tier

### Tier 3: The Budget Layer
**Long Island Affordable Web Design** (longislandaffordablewebdesign.com)
- Suffolk/Nassau/Hamptons. Transparent pricing. Contractors and service businesses.
- This is the Squarespace-alternative market. $500-$2,000.
- **Not your competitor.** These are your upgrade candidates.

**A Pre Design** (apredesign.com)
- "Affordable" East End web design. PHP-era site.
- Again, not competing — these clients are Taylor's prospects once they outgrow this.

### Tier 4: NYC Overflow
- Agencies like Dead On Design, high-end NYC shops
- $25,000-$100,000+ projects
- They don't have local presence. They fly in for meetings.
- **Taylor's angle:** Same quality, local presence, half the price. You're there.

## Where Taylor Sits

**Nobody in the Hamptons market is doing what Taylor does.** 

The local shops are WordPress/Wix/Squarespace. The NYC agencies charge 5-10x. There is a complete gap in the market for:
- Modern tech stack (Next.js, GSAP, Vercel)
- Premium animation and design
- AI-powered tools and automations
- Local presence + insider relationships
- $3,000-$25,000 price range

Taylor doesn't need to convince anyone the market exists. He needs to show up with his portfolio and let the work speak.
