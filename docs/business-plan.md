## 2. Service Tiers

### Tier 1 — Maison Starter | $3,000–$5,000

**Who it's for:** Solo operators, boutique shops, event businesses, artists, small hospitality. Businesses that need a credible web presence fast — not a $500 Squarespace template, but not a six-month build either.

**What's included:**
- 4–6 page Next.js site
- Mobile-first, responsive design
- GSAP entrance animations (subtle, on-brand — not flashy)
- Lenis smooth scroll
- Deployed on Vercel (fast, reliable, globally distributed)
- Contact form + basic SEO metadata
- 1 round of revisions
- 2-week delivery target

**What it's not:** CMS, e-commerce, custom integrations, AI tools.

**Positioning:** Better than Squarespace. Faster than an agency. Priced for the business that's serious but not ready to go all-in yet.

**Example clients:** Hampton Garden Party-style events businesses, local boutiques, therapists and wellness practitioners, solo attorneys or consultants.

**What's included:**
- 8–12 page Next.js site

**Positioning:** This is the tier where the site starts to feel like a brand asset rather than a business card. Roman Sanford and Hampton Garden Party are proof-of-concept at this level.

**Example clients:** Security consultancies (like Roman Sanford), established restaurants with a brand story, real estate agencies, premium event venues, medical/wellness practices.

## 3. Competitive Landscape

### Who's Operating in the Hamptons/Suffolk Web Design Market

**The Freelancer Layer ($500–$3,000)**
Local one-person shops and part-time freelancers doing Squarespace, Wix, and basic WordPress. This market is saturated and underpriced. Taylor doesn't compete here — he upgrades these clients.

**Regional Agencies — Southampton/East Hampton**
A handful of small agencies (2–8 person shops) operate in the Hamptons corridor. Based on local market research:
- *Typical pricing:* $5,000–$15,000 for custom sites
- *Tech stack:* WordPress, Webflow, occasionally Shopify
- *Design quality:* Competent but not exceptional — they're production shops, not design-forward studios
- *AI capability:* Essentially zero. None of these shops are building AI-powered tools or automations.

Known players in the space include shops like East End Web Design, local Webflow specialists, and generalist digital agencies in Riverhead and Southampton that serve a broad SMB market. None of them are doing Next.js + GSAP animation work at a premium tier.

**NYC Agencies (Overflow)**
High-end NYC digital agencies occasionally serve Hamptons clients — particularly luxury real estate, hospitality groups, and event brands. Pricing: $25,000–$100,000+. Taylor is positioned below this ceiling but with comparable design quality, and above the local competition in both aesthetics and technical capability.

**DIY (Squarespace/Wix)**
A huge portion of Hamptons businesses are still self-managing websites on consumer platforms. These are upgrade candidates — especially when Taylor can show them what $5K buys.

## 5. Growth Strategy

### Phase 1: Southampton Core (May–September 2026)

**Goal:** Establish Taylor Maison Creative as *the* premium web agency for Hamptons businesses.

**Tactics:**
- Work the Southampton network — Taylor's existing relationships are the pipeline. Howard Marks orbit, GVC connections, local business community.
- Target specific verticals with clear need and budget: security/private equity adjacent firms, luxury real estate, hospitality, events, wellness/medical.
- Attend local business events, the right dinner parties, the farmers market conversations that close deals in this market.
- Use the 4 existing portfolio pieces aggressively — Roman Sanford and RS Black in particular signal serious capability to serious clients.
- Price anchoring: always present Tier 3 first. Let clients self-select down.
- No cold outreach. Warm introductions only. This market runs on trust.

**Target by September:** 8–12 completed projects, 2–3 retainer clients, $150K+ billed.

### Phase 2: Suffolk County Expansion (September–December 2026)

**Goal:** Extend the brand east to Riverhead, west to Babylon — the full Suffolk business corridor.

**Tactics:**
- Suffolk has the density of businesses that the Hamptons doesn't — more volume, slightly lower budgets, but far less competition at the premium tier.
- Target: law firms, medical practices, specialty retail, manufacturing companies that need to modernize their digital presence.
- The Intelligent Systems pitch resonates hard here — Suffolk businesses are operationally lean and would pay for real automation.
- Consider a single co-working desk in Riverhead or Hauppauge to establish physical presence.
- Partner with local accountants, attorneys, and business brokers who refer professional services — they know which businesses have budget.

**Target by December:** 3–5 Suffolk County clients/month, retainer base growing to $8–12K/mo recurring.

### Phase 3: NYC Overflow (2027)

**Goal:** Position Taylor Maison as the agency that NYC businesses turn to for Hamptons-adjacent work — and eventually, premium NYC projects that don't require a Manhattan price tag.

**The overflow play:** NYC agencies occasionally have clients who want Hamptons-specific work — a summer venue, a Hamptons house brand, a seasonal popup. Taylor is the obvious referral partner. Build those agency relationships now.

**The pitch to NYC clients:** "You want NYC-quality work. You don't want to pay $150K for it. Call us."

**Tactics:**
- Cultivate 3–5 NYC agency relationships — offer referral fees (10–15% of project value).
- Attend 1–2 relevant NYC design/tech events per quarter (already have the network from film/TV production days).
- Target NYC clients with Hamptons touchpoints — the brands that summer there, the families that have houses there.
- The Genuine Undead comic IP project is a conversation starter in creative NYC circles.

**Target by mid-2027:** 2–3 NYC projects/quarter, blended monthly revenue $30–40K, 1 team hire (junior dev or designer).
