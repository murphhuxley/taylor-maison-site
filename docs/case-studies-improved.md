## 01. ROMAN SANFORD
*Web Design / Digital Security*
*2026*

### Current description (projects.ts)
> "Digital presence and brand identity for a security consultancy serving high-net-worth clients."

### Improved one-liner
> Roman Sanford protects the most valuable assets on earth. Their digital presence had to prove it.

### Improved brief
Roman Sanford serves high-net-worth individuals and families who require a different level of security expertise — private, precise, and rarely discussed. The challenge wasn't just design. It was engineering a digital presence that communicates elite competence to a clientele that reads status signals in milliseconds. The existing presence simply didn't hold up to that standard.

### Improved challenge copy
Security at this level operates on trust built long before a contract is signed. Every element of the design had to work as a credentialing mechanism — signaling authority without aggression, expertise without exposition, and discretion without hiding. We built a visual language borrowed from architecture and intelligence: clean structure, controlled hierarchy, and a restraint that says *we don't need to explain ourselves.*

### Services pull-quote (for sidebar)
> "Every design decision had to signal the same thing their team signals in person: we are exactly who we say we are."

### Why it works (internal note for Taylor to speak to)
The Roman Sanford project demonstrates the ability to build trust before a word is read. The typography, the dark palette, the sparse use of imagery — they all work as a credibility system. That's a harder design problem than making something beautiful. It's making something *believable.*

## 02. RS BLACK
*Web Design / Brand Identity*
*2026*

### Current description (projects.ts)
> "The classified tier. A dark, high-end digital experience for RS Black's premium intelligence program."

### Improved one-liner
> Some services don't advertise. They signal. RS Black required a digital experience that filtered for exactly the right audience.

### Improved brief
RS Black is the invitation-only tier of Roman Sanford's security operation — a program that doesn't recruit clients, it selects them. The digital experience needed to function as a filter: welcoming enough to intrigue, exclusive enough to deter the wrong inquiry. The brand language had to do the qualification before any human conversation began.

### Improved challenge copy
The design constraint was almost paradoxical: create a compelling online presence for something that specifically shouldn't be easily discoverable. We leaned into controlled sparsity — minimal text, near-black palette, precise typographic hierarchy that implies extreme focus. No stock photography. No corporate softness. The experience was designed to feel like a classified brief: everything you need, nothing you don't. 

The interface itself is the message: *you don't need a sales pitch when the product is this.*

### Services pull-quote
> "Not a website. A litmus test."

### Design note (for Taylor's verbal pitch)
RS Black is the hardest kind of design work: you're designing for someone who will never publicly praise it, and the success metric is whether the *wrong* person bounces and the *right* person leans in. That's a conversion problem disguised as a design problem.

## 03. GENUINE UNDEAD
*Creative Direction / IP Development*
*2025–2026*

### Current description (projects.ts)
> "A multi-century comic saga with full creative direction — from world-building to 3D web reader."

### Improved one-liner
> A comic universe spanning four millennia — from Ancient Egypt to cyberpunk Tokyo — required a digital world that felt just as vast.

### Improved brief
Genuine Undead isn't a comic series. It's an intellectual property architecture — narrative threads, visual systems, character universes, and world-building mechanics that needed to cohere across time periods, media formats, and community touchpoints. The brief covered everything from the story's internal logic to how a reader would experience it alone at 2am on a phone. Full creative direction meant owning all of it.

### Improved challenge copy
The technical and creative problems here were inseparable. The 3D parallax reader had to handle dense sequential art while making the experience feel cinematic rather than mechanical. The brand identity had to function across Ancient Egypt (hand-hewn, ochre and obsidian) and cyberpunk Tokyo (neon-veined, electric), while remaining unmistakably one universe. 

We built the narrative architecture first — identifying how the centuries connected thematically — and let that structure dictate the visual language. The result is a platform where the design and the story reinforce each other at every scroll.

### Services pull-quote
> "Building a universe requires the same discipline as building a brand: every decision either reinforces the internal logic or breaks it."

## 04. HAMPTON GARDEN PARTY
*Web Design / Brand Strategy*
*2026*

### Current description (projects.ts)
> "Brand identity and digital home for a Hamptons-based event and garden company."

### Improved one-liner
> A Hamptons garden company needed a brand that felt like a perfect Sunday afternoon in June. That's harder than it sounds.

### Improved brief
Hampton Garden Party exists at the intersection of two of the East End's most competitive service categories — events and landscape design — and needed a brand that felt native to both without being generic to either. The brief: capture the sensory experience of a Hamptons summer garden the way a song captures a mood. Warm, specific, unhurried.

### Improved challenge copy
The hardest design challenge is designing *for a feeling.* Hampton Garden Party's identity had to evoke dappled afternoon light, the particular green of midsummer privet hedges, and the specific social ease of people who've been summering on the East End for generations. 

We anchored the palette in the garden itself — a dusty sage-green that reads as both lush and refined — and built the typography system around Hamptons editorial aesthetics: generous whitespace, serif accents in restrained use, and a visual rhythm that feels unhurried. The website needed to make visitors *slow down.* In a market where everyone is competing on urgency, Hampton Garden Party competes on atmosphere.

### Services pull-quote
> "In the Hamptons, the aesthetic IS the product. We designed for the feeling first, the function second."
